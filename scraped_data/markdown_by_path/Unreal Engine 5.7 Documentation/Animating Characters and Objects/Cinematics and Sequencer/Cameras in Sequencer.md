## Overview

Sequencer includes powerful camera tools, enabling you to achieve your filmmaking goals in Unreal Engine. The following pages provide an overview of the different ways you can use cameras and enhance their behavior.

[![Cine Camera Actor](images/static/document_list/empty_thumbnail.svg)

Cine Camera Actor

The Cine Camera Actor is used as the primary camera type for filming cinematic content in Unreal Engine.](/documentation/en-us/unreal-engine/cinematic-cameras-in-unreal-engine)
[![Camera Rigs](images/static/document_list/empty_thumbnail.svg)

Camera Rigs

Film with real-world techniques in Unreal Engine by using Camera Rigs.](/documentation/en-us/unreal-engine/camera-jibs-and-dollies-in-unreal-engine)
[![Camera Shakes](images/static/document_list/empty_thumbnail.svg)

Camera Shakes

Create camera shake effects in Unreal Engine.](/documentation/en-us/unreal-engine/camera-shakes-in-unreal-engine)
[![Cinematic Viewport](images/static/document_list/empty_thumbnail.svg)

Cinematic Viewport

Use the Cinematic Viewport to add filmmaking controls in the viewport.](/documentation/en-us/unreal-engine/cinematic-viewport-controls-in-unreal-engine)
[![Image Plate](images/static/document_list/empty_thumbnail.svg)

Image Plate

Play full screen videos and image sequences on your cameras using Image Plates.](/documentation/en-us/unreal-engine/full-screen-movies-in-unreal-engine)
